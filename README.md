## The Short Version

I'm a product-minded engineer who likes building systems with a real job to do.

Not just dashboards. Not just landing pages. The kind of software that has to understand roles, records, approvals, uploads, audit trails, edge cases, and the quiet chaos of actual organizations.

I move comfortably from interface to infrastructure: shaping the product, designing the workflow, modeling the data, building the API, polishing the UI, and wiring in AI where it genuinely makes the work lighter.

## The Work I Keep Choosing

Most of my best work lives in private repos, but the pattern is pretty clear:

- **Kazispace**: digitizing informal workforce discovery, hiring, and coordination in Rwanda.
- **CareLogic**: EMR, clinic management, inventory, and healthcare operations tooling.
- **Posy**: AI-assisted form, document, and workflow infrastructure.
- **Licensing and compliance systems**: application intake, document review, internal approvals, audit trails, and decision workflows.
- **Optic**: optical shop SaaS foundations, including inventory, prescriptions, lab work, tenancy, sales, and reporting.
- **Dukani**: mobile-first product thinking around inventory and stock workflows.

That mix is my lane: software for serious workflows, built with enough taste that people do not feel punished for using it.

## How I Build

I like systems that feel simple because the hard parts were handled with care.

- Interfaces that make complicated work feel organized.
- APIs with clear contracts, predictable behavior, and room to grow.
- Data models that respect the real workflow instead of forcing the workflow to fake simplicity.
- AI features that earn their keep by saving time, reducing mistakes, or unlocking better decisions.
- Products that can survive handoff, iteration, and real users.

## A Small Engineering Philosophy

Build the system like someone will depend on it on a busy Monday morning.

Make it fast enough to trust.
Make it clear enough to teach.
Make it boring where boring is a gift.
Make it beautiful where beauty reduces friction.
